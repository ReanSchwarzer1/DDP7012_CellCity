Cell City

"The cell is a city. You are its officials. But something has gotten past the membrane..."

What Is Cell City?

Cell City is a board game about a living cell. You and your friends each play as a different organelle, a tiny part of the cell that does a specific job. You work together like a team running a city.

The Twist: One player is secretly a virus. They look like a normal organelle, but they're actually trying to destroy the cell from the inside, by wasting resources, letting trash pile up, or quietly sabotaging the team.

Game Information

Players4-6

GenreTeam game with a secret traitor

Win Conditions

  • Healthy Players Win If: The Clock reaches Round 8 (Survival) OR all Viral Agents are correctly exiled. The player with the most LP is the Grand Winner.
  • The Virus Wins If: Total Waste on the board reaches 12 (Toxic Overload), ATP hits 0 and no Glucose is left anywhere (Energy Death), OR 2 or more Healthy players are wrongly exiled (Autoimmune Collapse).

The 5 Core Roles (+1 Expansion)

The Director (Nucleus)

What you do: You control the blueprints. When anyone wants to build a protein, they need an Instruction Card, and you're the one who hands them out.

Your power: When you draw Instruction Cards, draw 3 and keep 2. Everyone else draws 2 and keeps 1.

SYS.NOTE: The nucleus holds DNA, the instructions for building every protein the cell needs.

The Engineer (Mitochondria)

What you do: You turn Glucose into ATP (energy). Without you, nobody can do anything; every action in the game costs ATP.

Your power: When you convert Glucose into ATP, you make 1 extra ATP. (You get 4 ATP from 2 Glucose. Everyone else gets 3.)

SYS.NOTE: Mitochondria perform cellular respiration, turning sugar into usable energy.

The Builder (ER + Ribosomes)

What you do: You build proteins. When a player has an Instruction Card and the right resources, you process it at the ER to earn Life Points.

Your power: When you build a protein, roll a die. On a 3 or higher, it's a High-Quality Protein, worth 2 extra LP.

SYS.NOTE: Rough ER has ribosomes on its surface and assembles proteins from amino acids.

The Recycler (Lysosome)

What you do: You clean up Waste. Waste accumulates each round, and if it gets too high, the cell dies.

Your power: Every round, you automatically remove 2 Waste from the board at no cost and with no action needed.

SYS.NOTE: Lysosomes break down waste, damaged parts, and even invading bacteria.

The Gatekeeper (Cell Membrane)

What you do: You control the door. Each round, you draw resource tokens from the Supply and decide who gets what.

Your power: You draw 2 extra resource tokens from the Supply each round compared to the base amount.

SYS.NOTE: The cell membrane is selectively permeable; it decides what enters and exits the cell.

The Courier (Golgi Apparatus)

Only used in 5-6 player games.

What you do: You deliver finished proteins.

Your power: When you deliver a protein, you and the player who receives it both gain +1 LP.

SYS.NOTE: The Golgi apparatus packages proteins into vesicles and ships them where needed.

Quick-Start Guide

SETUP: Deal each player 1 Role Card (face-up) and 1 Allegiance Card (face-down, keep secret). Set out tokens. Shuffle both decks.

EACH ROUND (4 steps):

  1. Flip an Event Card. Do what it says.
  2. The Gatekeeper hands out Glucose and Amino Acids.
  3. Each player takes a turn: pick 2 actions (make energy, get blueprints, build proteins, clean waste, or trade). Then gain 1 Waste.
  4. Check: Waste ≥ 12? ATP = 0? → Virus wins. Clock hits 8? → Healthy wins.

THE SECRET: One of you is a Virus. They win if the cell dies. Spend 1 Immunity Token to accuse someone, but if you're wrong, the cell takes damage.

The Cell Scan (Catching the Virus)

If you think someone is a virus, you can accuse them. This is the loudest, most dramatic part of the game.

  • Spend 1 Immunity Token and point at someone. Say: "I think YOU are the virus."
  • 30-second argument. The accuser says why. The accused defends themselves. Everyone else talks.
  • Vote. Everyone shows a thumbs-up (exile) or a thumbs-down (safe). The majority wins. Ties = safe.
  • Reveal:
    • If they ARE a Virus: They're out! Remove 3 Waste from the board.
    • If they're Healthy: They're out! Add 3 Waste to the board. The accuser loses 2 LP. (This represents an autoimmune response).

The Event Deck

Adrenaline Rush

The Engineer makes +2 extra ATP this round.

Diffusion Gradient

The Gatekeeper draws 4 extra resources to hand out.

Active Transport

Anyone can trade 1 ATP for 3 Amino Acids this round.

Immune Boost

Every player gains 1 Immunity Token.

Viral Protease

Every player loses 1 Protein (if they have one).

Lysosomal Leak

Everyone gains +2 Waste.

Ribosome Strike

Building proteins costs +1 Amino Acid this round.

Thermal Stress

Every player discards 1 resource of their choice.

Membrane Breach

The Gatekeeper must spend 2 ATP to seal it, or everyone gains +3 Waste.

What Kids Learn (Without Realising It)

Biology isn't a quiz. It IS the game. Every mechanic maps to a real cell process:

What Happens in the Game What It Teaches
The Engineer turns Glucose into ATP Cellular respiration, mitochondria convert sugar into energy
The Gatekeeper decides who gets resources The cell membrane is selectively permeable
The Builder makes proteins from Amino Acids Ribosomes and the ER assemble proteins from amino acid chains
Waste builds up and can kill the cell Apoptosis, programmed cell death from accumulated damage
The Recycler cleans waste Lysosomes break down waste and damaged cell parts
The Virus pretends to be an organelle Viruses hijack cells by disguising themselves and exploiting cellular machinery
A wrong accusation damages the cell In autoimmune diseases, the immune system attacks the body's own cells
Everyone depends on each other Organelles work together; no single part can keep a cell alive alone
